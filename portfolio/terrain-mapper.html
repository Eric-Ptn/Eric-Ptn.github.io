Overview

Inspired by my experience at UBC AeroDesign and a desire to test the skills I've learned so far, I began to independently design a canard, twin vertical tail, pusher aircraft configuration during the summer of 2022. This aircraft will later be equipped with sensors and an onboard system to provide video feedback and map the terrain underneath it. So far, I have completed airfoil analysis and determined a 3D wing configuration, conducted stability analysis, and modelled the entire plane in SolidWorks with DFA/M considerations in mind. Next steps are to manufacture the first prototype and develop the necessary software.

Design

  • Used CFD tools and XFLR to analyze aerodynamics
  • Determined dynamic stability performance from root locus graphs of longitudinal and lateral response modes
  • Created SolidWorks model of design

Results

  • Achieved wing planform efficiency of 0.986 without sacrificing manufacturability
  • Despite the canard configuration, all response modes are damped and dynamically stable
  • Wing and tail stall characteristics are coordinated
