Overview

UBC AeroDesign is an engineering student team that designs, builds, and flies payload-lifting, radio-controlled fixed-wing airplanes to compete in the international SAE Aero Design Collegiate Series. The 2022 Advanced Class competition featured a firefighting-inspired mission profile. As a part of the Advanced Airfoils Team, I helped conduct airfoil analysis, design a 3D wing configuration, model the vertical stabilizer and size the wing spar to meet the competition requirements.

Design

  • Modelled the vertical stabilizer in SolidWorks, added to the plane assembly in PDM
  • Configured the wing spar by using a strength-to-weight material index and finding an optimal area moment of inertia

Results

  • Reduced assembly time and minimized human error through implementing DFA/M principles
  • Wing spar was reused through multiple design iterations, reducing costs and ordering times
  • The UBC Advanced Team earned 5th place overall in the 2022 competition
