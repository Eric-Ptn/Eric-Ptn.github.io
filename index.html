Hey there! I'm Eric Petersen, a first year applied science student at the University of British Columbia.

About

I'm an enthusiastic engineering student accepted with early entrance into the University of British Columbia, one of the top 40 universities in the world. In high school I was a part of the University Transition Program, a radically accelerated program for gifted adolescents, where I received Outstander Scholar recognition in math, physics, English, calculus, and history.

In addition to my learning in school, I participate in several academic competitions, including the Waterloo math contests and science fairs, performing successfully in many of these events. Check out the academics page for more details

On top of academics, I play piano, program Arduinos, make music, and even create websites (wow). Apart from pursuing hobbies and passions, I also love to hang out with my brother and my friends during my free time.

Recent volunteering & work experience:

Assisted with recruitment and onboarding of new volunteers at COSMIC Medical, an open-source medical innovation platform created in response to the Covid-19 pandemic.

Joined the Project Cover group during the Covid-19 pandemic to help promote the design of an inexpensive ventilator for the use of low-resource countries. Created this project video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=acPjWKjcIzM.

Designed a 3D CAD model for the containment of medical devices to help relieve stress from kids going to the hospital.

Instructed Level 5 piano lessons as a private piano teacher.
